Module 01 — Foundation

Building Effective
Project Teams

In small organisations, the team IS the project. Understanding how teams form, conflict, and perform is foundational to everything else.

🇮🇪 Irish Context

Over 350,000 people volunteer in Ireland each year (CSO, 2023). Managing volunteers requires fundamentally different approaches to motivation, recognition, and flexibility than managing paid staff.

🎬Tuckman's Stages ExplainedVIDEO
▶
Tuckman's Stages of Group Development
Mind Tools · 5 min

An illustrated overview of each stage with practical scenarios — ideal for sharing at project kickoff with your team.

Tuckman's Five Stages 1

Stage 1
Forming
Politeness and uncertainty. Clarify roles, set ground rules, and establish expectations. In charity settings, this is where volunteers decide if they're committed.
Stage 2
Storming
Conflict emerges as people assert working styles. This is normal and necessary — channel it through structured retrospectives and active listening.
Stage 3
Norming
Shared norms develop. Formalise agreements on decision-making, conflict resolution, and workload sharing.
Stage 4
Performing
High trust, high output. Shift from directive leadership to facilitation. Protect the team's focus from scope creep.
Stage 5
Adjourning
Celebrate achievements, capture lessons learned, conduct proper handoffs. In volunteer teams, a poor adjournment means volunteers don't return next time.
🎭

Belbin Team Roles 2

Belbin identified 9 team roles from Coordinator to Completer-Finisher. In small teams one person often plays multiple roles. Assess your team at belbin.com.

🛡️

Psychological Safety 3

Edmondson (1999) demonstrated that teams where members feel safe to raise problems without blame dramatically outperform those that don't. Create safety before demanding performance.

💻

Remote & Hybrid Teams

Ireland's Right to Request Remote Work Act (2024) gives employees new rights. Build a team charter covering working hours, communication norms, and decision rights from day one.

Module 02 — Foundation

Risk
Management

Risk is "an uncertain event that, if it occurs, has a positive or negative effect on project objectives" (PMI PMBOK® 7th ed.) 4 — note that risks can be opportunities too.

⚡ Irish-Specific Risks

GDPR / DPC enforcement · Planning permission delays (An Bord Pleanála) · Grant conditionality (Pobal, Tusla, LEADER) · Brexit supply chain disruption · Volunteer turnover · Cash flow gaps between grant tranches

The 4Ts of Risk Response

🛑 Terminate

Eliminate by removing the activity. Often right for high-impact, high-likelihood risks in underfunded projects.

↩️ Transfer

Insurance, sub-contracts, indemnity clauses. Public liability insurance is required by law for Irish public events.

🔧 Treat

Reduce likelihood or impact. Cross-training, better contracts, stronger stakeholder communication.

✅ Tolerate

Accept low-impact risks. Document acceptance and monitor. Don't over-engineer responses to minor risks.

Module 03 — People

Leadership

"Management is doing things right; leadership is doing the right things."— Peter Drucker (1967), The Effective Executive 5

The most technically perfect project plan will fail without effective leadership. In Ireland's SME and charity sectors, project managers are frequently player-coaches — doing the work while also leading it.

Greenleaf (1970) 6 coined servant leadership — the leader exists to serve the team. For charity PMs working with volunteers, ask: "What do you need to succeed?" rather than "Here's what I need from you."

Practical applications: removing blockers; advocating for resources upward; celebrating others' contributions publicly; taking personal responsibility for failures.

Bass & Avolio (1994) 7 describe transformational leaders as inspiring through vision, challenging assumptions, and developing individuals. Highly effective when your project requires culture change — new systems, digitalisation, or shifts in service delivery.

Hersey & Blanchard (1969) 8 argued effective leaders adapt based on team member competence and commitment: S1 Directing → S2 Coaching → S3 Supporting → S4 Delegating. Adjust per person and per task, not per project.

Goleman (1995) 9 demonstrated that EQ — self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, social skills — is a stronger predictor of leadership effectiveness than IQ. In small organisations where relationships are intense, EQ is arguably the PM's most critical capability.

Module 04 — People

Ethics in
Project Management

Ethics are not a compliance checkbox — they are the foundation of trust with your beneficiaries, funders, and community. For charities, ethical failure can be existential.

🎯
Responsibility

Own your decisions and consequences — including mistakes. Report problems early; don't bury them.

🤝
Respect

Honour diverse perspectives. In multilingual Irish communities, this requires active, deliberate effort.

⚖️
Fairness

Transparent, unbiased decisions — especially in procurement and hiring. Document every choice.

💡
Honesty

Accurate reporting to funders. No spin on outcomes. Charities must report failures alongside successes.

"Ethics is not what we say — it's what we do when no one is watching."— Treviño & Nelson, Managing Business Ethics, 7th ed. (2017) 13
🇮🇪 GDPR & The Data Protection Commission

Ireland's DPC is one of Europe's most active GDPR regulators. Any project handling personal data requires: a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) · a legal basis for processing · a data retention policy · a 72-hour breach notification procedure. Consult dataprotection.ie for official guidance.

Module 05 — Foundation

Sustainability &
the Environment

Ireland has legally binding targets — 51% emissions reduction by 2030. Every organisation, regardless of size, has a role. Projects are the mechanism through which organisations change.

Triple Bottom Line — Elkington (1994) 14

Sustainable projects balance Planet (environmental impact), People (social impact), and Profit / Purpose (financial viability). Report against all three — not just finances.

🇮🇪 Climate Action Plan 2024

Ireland's Climate Action and Low Carbon Development Act 2021 requires all publicly-funded organisations to embed climate considerations into planning. Apply energy efficiency first; measure and report your project's carbon footprint.

Module 06 — Foundation

Governance

Good governance ensures your organisation operates accountably, transparently, and in pursuit of your mission. In Ireland, governance has specific legal dimensions for charities and companies alike.

The 6 Principles of the Charity Governance Code 15

Principle 1
Advancing your charitable purpose
Every project must demonstrably serve your charitable objects. Mission drift is a governance failure.
Principle 2
Behaving with integrity
Board members act in the charity's interest, manage conflicts of interest, and set the ethical tone.
Principle 3
Leading people
Effective governance of staff and volunteers — proper HR policies, safeguarding, dignity at work.
Principle 4
Exercising control
Financial controls, risk management, legal compliance. The Board is ultimately responsible for all project finances.
Principle 5
Working effectively
Board composition, induction, evaluation, and succession planning. A dysfunctional Board kills good projects.
Principle 6
Being accountable and transparent
Annual reports, audited accounts, and public communications. Charities must file annual returns with the Charities Regulator.
🏛️ CRO & Charities Regulator — Key Obligations

Companies: Annual return to CRO within 28 days of AGM. Late filing incurs penalties and potential strike-off.

Charities: Register with Charities Regulator; file Annual Report within 10 months of financial year end; maintain a Register of Interests for trustees.

Both: GDPR registration with DPC; Revenue CHY status for tax exemption.

Strategy &

A project without strategic alignment is busywork. Connect every project to your organisation's long-term mission using these proven frameworks.

→

Long-term societal change

SWOT Analysis 17

Strengths

Internal positive factors. What does your team do exceptionally well? What assets and relationships do you have?

Weaknesses

Internal negative factors. Skill gaps, resource constraints, capacity limits. Be brutally honest — this is where most plans fail.

Opportunities

External positive factors. New EU funding rounds, political support, demographic changes, digital tools.

Threats

External negative factors. Funding cuts, policy changes, competition, inflation, volunteer fatigue.

OKRs in Practice 19

🎯 Objective: Transform digital access for older adults in our county
KR1Train 150 people aged 65+ in basic smartphone skills by Q3
KR2Achieve 80% participant satisfaction in post-course survey
KR3Partner with 3 local libraries to sustain drop-in support beyond project end
Module 08 — Foundation

Planning &
Implementation

Failing to plan is planning to fail — but over-planning is also a trap. Plan just enough to control the project while remaining adaptive.

The Project Lifecycle

01
Initiation
Project brief, business case, stakeholder analysis, initial risk assessment. Output: Project Charter.
02
Planning
WBS, Gantt chart, resource plan, budget, communication plan, risk register. Output: Project Management Plan.
03
Execution
Delivering the work. Managing the team, tracking progress, handling change requests.
04
Monitoring & Control
Status reports, KPI tracking, budget vs actual, issue log. Runs parallel to execution.
05
Closure
Lessons learned, final funder report, handover documentation, team recognition, financial audit.

Module 09 — People

Stakeholder
Engagement

Projects fail because of people, not plans. Identifying who matters, what they care about, and how to engage them is one of the most underdeveloped skills in small organisations.

The Power-Interest Matrix 21 22

LOW INTEREST
HIGH INTEREST
HIGH POWER
Keep Satisfied

Senior funders, Board members, Government bodies. High power, may not monitor closely. Keep informed; seek input on key decisions.

Manage Closely

Key funders with monitoring roles, Board sponsor, local authorities. Your most important stakeholders — regular updates, co-design, early warning of problems.

LOW POWER
Monitor

General public, peripheral groups. Keep informed through newsletters. Watch for changes in interest level.

Keep Informed

Service users, community members. Often most affected by your project. High ethical obligation to involve them meaningfully in decision-making.

🇮🇪 Key Irish Stakeholder Bodies

Pobal — Manages government & EU social inclusion funding · LEADER — Rural development through Local Action Groups · Tusla — Child & Family Agency · Local Authorities — Community grants, LECP alignment · LCDC / PPN — Community voice in local government

Module 10 — Methods

Project Management
Methodologies

There is no universally "best" methodology. The right approach depends on your project's complexity, certainty of requirements, team size, and funder expectations.

Waterfall / Traditional PM

Sequential phases — initiation → planning → execution → close. Best where requirements are well understood and change is unlikely.

✅ Use when

Construction projects · Events with fixed dates · Regulatory compliance · Prescriptive funder requirements

⚠️ Limitations

Inflexible to change. Problems discovered late are expensive to fix. Not suited to digital or innovation projects.

▶
Plain English comparison · 8 min

A clear breakdown of when to use each approach — ideal for explaining your methodology to board members or funders.

Agile Project Management

From the Agile Manifesto (2001) 25 — iterative delivery, collaboration, and responding to change over following a fixed plan.

✅ Use when

Website or app development · Service design · Innovation pilots · Projects where requirements evolve through user feedback

  • Sprint: 1–4 week cycles of Plan → Build → Review → Retrospect
  • Backlog: prioritised using MoSCoW — Must / Should / Could / Won't
  • Daily standup: 15-minute check-in replaces multi-hour meetings
  • OGCIO has published an Agile Framework for Irish public bodies
🆓 Free Agile Tools

Trello — Free Kanban board
Jira — Free up to 10 users
GitHub Projects — Free for small teams
Physical post-it wall — Still highly effective for co-located teams

PRINCE2 26

PRojects IN Controlled Environments — a process-based framework widely used across Ireland, particularly in public sector and EU-funded projects.

✅ Use when

EU structural funds (ERDF, ESF) · Large public sector projects · High governance and audit trail requirements · Bidding for HSE or Government contracts

7 Principles of PRINCE2

  • Continued business justification — stop if no longer worthwhile
  • Learn from experience — built in, not bolted on
  • Defined roles and responsibilities — no ambiguity
  • Manage by stages — formal review before proceeding
  • Manage by exception — escalate only when tolerances breach
  • Focus on products — define the deliverable first
  • Tailor to the project — scale it, don't skip it

Lean Project Management 27

From Toyota's production system (Ohno, 1988) — maximise value for the end user while eliminating waste. Now widely applied in Irish healthcare and charities.

✅ Lean in Ireland

The HSE has applied Lean extensively across hospitals. Cope Galway and Peter McVerry Trust have adopted Lean principles to reduce client waiting times.

8 Wastes in Services

Overproduction

Reports nobody reads

Waiting

Approval bottlenecks

Over-processing

More than client needs

Defects

Errors requiring rework

🏆 Best Practice Recommendation

Overall project: PRINCE2-style governance — satisfies funders and auditors
Within each stage: Agile sprints — allows the team to adapt and learn from users
Outcome measurement: Logic Model + OKRs — bridges activities to long-term impact

Module 11 — Digital

AI in Project
Management

Free and low-cost AI tools are transforming what small teams can accomplish — if used thoughtfully, ethically, and with awareness of Ireland's regulatory environment.

📝
Meeting Summaries

Otter.ai, Fireflies, Microsoft Copilot — transcribe and summarise meetings automatically, saving 30–60 min of admin per session.

✍️
Grant Writing

AI drafts first versions of grant applications, funder reports, and communications. Humans refine, verify facts, and add authentic voice.

📊
Data Analysis

ChatGPT with Code Interpreter or Copilot in Excel analyse survey data, produce charts, and identify patterns in your outcome data.

🗓️
Schedule Optimisation

Reclaim.ai and Motion automatically schedule tasks around meetings, protecting deep work time for small teams with complex diaries.

🔍
Research & Synthesis

Perplexity.ai and Claude excel at synthesising large documents — useful for literature reviews, policy analysis, and benchmarking.

🌐
Translation

DeepL and Google Translate enable multilingual communication — valuable for projects serving Ireland's diverse migrant communities.

🇮🇪 Irish & EU AI Regulation
  • National AI Strategy 2021 30 — "AI — Here for Good" — trustworthy, human-centred AI for Ireland
  • EU AI Act (2024) 31 — classifies AI by risk level; high-risk applications face strict requirements
  • DPC Guidance on AI & GDPR — do not input client personal data into public AI tools
  • Pobal Digital Support Scheme — funding available for charities adopting AI-assisted services
⚠️ Critical Limitations
  • AI "hallucinations" — always verify facts independently before publishing
  • GDPR — never input personal data of clients or beneficiaries into public AI tools
  • Bias — AI outputs reflect training data biases; review critically for Irish context
  • Skills atrophy — use AI to supplement, not replace, human expertise and judgement
  • Check funder policy on AI-generated content in reports before submitting
